LOOK SCHEDULING ALGORITHM:

The LOOK scheduling algorithm is also known as the elevator algorithm because it works in a similar fashion to an elevator. The head of the disk moves from the outermost track to the innermost track and back to the outermost track, servicing requests as it moves. When there are no more requests in one direction, the head changes direction and begins servicing requests in the opposite direction.

C-LOOK SCHEDULING ALGORITHM:

The C-LOOK scheduling algorithm is an improvement over the LOOK scheduling algorithm. It improves disk performance by reducing the average seek time and the number of head movements. The C-LOOK algorithm works in the same way as the LOOK algorithm, but it only services requests in one direction. When it reaches the innermost track, it jumps to the outermost track and continues servicing requests in the same direction.
